Dorothy was feeling lost in life. Unhappy with her career, she longed for a job in technology where she could learn new things and be creative. But it felt like that dream was something over the rainbow. One day, Dorothy decided to try her hand at coding. As she typed the strange syntax into her computer, she felt herself being transported to a new land.

"Where am I?" Dorothy wondered. A popup alert informed her that she was in the Land of Technology. "But how do I get home?" she asked. The popup alert pointed towards a path paved with bricks made of ones and zeros. It was the Path of Learning and this was the way home. Dorothy was nervous. The Path of Learning had no end in sight. Suddenly a black hoodie appeared at her feet. She put it on and instantly felt better. She began walking down the path.

The Path of Learning was exciting at first. But the more she traveled the more she realized there was so much more to go. She began to feel very overwhelmed. "Will I ever reach the end of this path?" Dorothy asked. An unexpected voice behind her answered, "you will never succeed on the Path of Learning!" Dorothy turned to see who had said those awful words. It was the Wicked Witch of Imposter Syndrome!

Dorothy turned away from the witch and ran as fast as she could. When she stopped to catch her breath she saw a man standing in a field. Dorothy waved. The man walked over and said, "Hi, I am Tim Berners-Lee. I see you are on the Path of Learning. I've traveled this path myself. You look like you could use some help." Dorothy didn't want to bother the man but she did need help. She accepted and he joined her on the Path of Learning.

Dorothy told the man about the Wicked Witch of Imposter Syndrome. "Oh yes, the witch can be a problem for everyone in the Land of Technology. Even those of us who helped to build the path." He went on to tell Dorothy about his life of learning. Tim and his team created HTML and the World Wide Web back in 1989. HTML is a collection of tags that are read by the computer allowing text to appear in the web browser.

Dorothy and Tim continued down the Path of Learning. The path was rarely straightforward and sometimes seemed to take them back the way they came. Tim told Dorothy of many others who traveled the Path. "In fact," Tim said, "we are near one of my collegues. Did you notice the flowers have become more colorful?" Dorothy saw Tim was right. The Land of Technology seemed to appear much more pleasing. "Hello Håkon!" said Tim.

Håkon Wium Lie joined Dorothy and Tim. Håkon had helped Tim build the parts of the path many years ago. Håkon's speciality was CSS. This is the software that added styles to HTML. Styles give the Land of Technology it's color and design. Dorothy was glad for her new friends. She suspected that they knew how to keep the Wicked Witch away. As she expressed her gratitude, the trio encountered a popup alert that blocked the path. "Oh no, what do we do?" Dorothy exclaimed.

Since Tim and Håkon had been on the path many times, they had seen the popup before. They knew the best thing is to ask for help from someone who was familar with this section of path. They took a deep breath and yelled, "Brendan!" Out of the woods came Brendan Eich the creator of JavaScript, the technology behind the popup alert. Brendan helped the team understand how to get passed. Then, Brendan joined the group and they all talked more about the Land of Technology.

Dorothy had seen something on the popup alert she didn't understand. "What is localhost?" Her friends explained that localhost is the home IP address. "IP addresses are how information is found in the Land of Technology." They told her the IP address for home is 127.0.0.1. "Is that how I get home?" Dorothy asked, "I have to find the IP address?" Her friends exchanged glances. Dorothy felt something was wrong. "Dorothy, it isn't that easy," Tim said. Dorothy felt her heart sink.

"Will I be on the path forever? Will I ever get home?" Tim, Håkon, and Brendan sat down on the side of the path. "Dorothy," they said, "you are home. The Land of Technology is part of us. It is home. The Path of Learning is one we walk all our lives. It is what makes life interesting, it makes us who we are!" Dorothy thought for a moment. "What about the witch?" she asked. "The Wicked Witch of Imposter Syndrome is only as scary as you let her be." Dorothy realized they were right. She was home! And with her new friends to help her on the path, she was happy.
